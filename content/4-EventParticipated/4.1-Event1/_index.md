# Reflection Report: "FCJ Community Day"

### Purpose of the Event

- Share best practices in modern application design
- Introduce DDD and event-driven architecture
- Guide the selection of suitable compute services
- Introduce AI tools that support the development lifecycle

### Speakers

- **Tinh Truong** - Platforms Engineer, GoTymeX
- **Anh Pham** - Cloud Consultant, G-AsiaPacific Vietnam
- **Thinh Nguyen** - DevOps Engineer, FCAJ
- **Thao Nguyen** - GenAI Engineer, VIB
- **Mai Nguyen** - GenAI Engineer, VIB
- **Uyen Le** - GenAI Engineer, VIB
- **Duc Dao** - Solutions Architect, Cloud Kinetics
- **Vy Lam** - Senior Business Systems Analyst, VPBank

### Key Highlights

#### Context is everything: How AI can truly serve you

- Why AI fails without context, and what “context” really means
- How AI is evolving from prompt to memory
- How better context leads to better results
- Career perspectives and how students can start building with AI and Q&A

#### Amazon Quick’s friendly AI assistant

- Quick Chat Agent: An AI assistant that helps explore data and analyze insights
- Quick Flows: Create intelligent workflows using natural language without programming
- Quick Spaces: A shared collaboration space that turns personal insights into team knowledge
- Quick Sight: Build dashboards and reports from raw data using natural language

#### From Edge to Origin: CloudFront as your platform

- Amazon CloudFront for all types of workloads
- Cost optimization with Amazon CloudFront
- Security capabilities
- Improved reliability with Amazon CloudFront
- Improved performance with Amazon CloudFront

#### 36 hours with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho from idea to reality

- Why we joined LotusHacks
- From zero to idea – The brainstorming journey
- Identifying the problem and shaping UTMorpho
- Building under pressure – A 36-hour development sprint
- Challenges, failures, and turning points
- UTMorpho – Product overview and demo
- Key lessons and next steps

#### The nondeterminism of “deterministic” LLM settings

- How LLMs choose the next token
- Assumption: Temperature = 0 ensures determinism
- Reality: Inference-time optimizations can show the opposite
- Practical impact
- Mitigation strategies

#### Enterprise multi-agent systems: A credit-scoring case for startups

- Structural incompatibility between banking systems and startup data
- Single Agent: When to use it and when not to
- Multi-Agent model
- Design of a virtual Credit Committee
- Guardrails and compliance
- Operational ROI and implementation roadmap

### What I Learned

- Through this event, I learned that for AI to work effectively, it needs clear context, suitable data, and the right system design aligned with the goal. In addition, I gained a better understanding of technologies such as Amazon Quick, CloudFront, LLMs, and multi-agent systems, and how they are applied in practice.

### Application to Work

- The knowledge from this event can be applied to building AI systems that support data analysis, automate workflows, and improve cloud application performance. In particular, the content on CloudFront, multi-agent systems, and LLMs gives me more direction when developing projects related to AI and AWS.

### Experience in the Event

- The event gave me many practical perspectives through the speakers’ presentations and product demos. I found the content very useful, easy to connect to student projects, and motivating for further learning and future AI solution development.
